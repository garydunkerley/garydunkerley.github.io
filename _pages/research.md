I am interested in knots, links, and surfaces in 4-manifolds. My current work concerns minimality of knot cobordisms.

### Squeezed knots and satellite operations (in preparation)
Feller, Lewark, and Lobb define a squeezed knot to be any knot that arises as a slice of a genus-minimizing connected, oriented cobordism between a positive torus knot and a negative torus knot. Give a squeezed knot, they show that it satisfies the curious property that all slice torus invariants (such as s/2 and tau) agree on it.
We give a classification result for cables of positive (and negative) braid knots and leverage this into a proof that the cable of any squeezed knot is squeezed. We also show that for any integer m and any knot in the 3-sphere, its associated (m,n)-cable knot will be squeezed provided n is sufficiently large (or small). This gives an explanation to an observation of Van Cott.
