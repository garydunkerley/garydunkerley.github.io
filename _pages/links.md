### Useful
- [Signal](https://signal.org/#signal) - a beautiful, easy-to-use, open-source messaging app built with security in mind, because privacy is a human right
- [Overleaf](https://www.overleaf.com/) - a web-based LaTeX compiler designed for collaboration
- [OEIS](https://oeis.org/) - The Online Encyclopedia of Integer Sequences
- [KnotInfo](https://knotinfo.math.indiana.edu/) - an excellent resource for data on knots up to twelve crossings
- [SnapPy](https://snappy.math.uic.edu/) - a program for studying the geometry of hyperbolic manifolds courtesy of Marc Culler, Nathan Dunfield, and Matthias Goerner
- [SeifertView](https://www.win.tue.nl/~vanwijk/seifertview/) - Jack van Wijk's software for visualizing Seifert surfaces
- [q.uiver](https://q.uiver.app/) - an in-browser app for making commutative diagrams, exports to LaTeX!
- [Detexify](http://detexify.kirelabs.org/classify.html) - trades hand-drawn symbols for LaTeX commands

### Aesthetic 
- [Cara Dunkerley's site](https://caradunkerley.com/) - my sibling makes art, check it out!
- [Simulations of strange attractors](http://www.stevejtrettel.site/code/strangeAttractor.html) - Steve Trettel's amazing strange attractor simulator
- [Ray-traced simulations of the eight Thurston geometries](http://www.3-dimensional.space/) - a beautiful project by Remi Coulon, Sabetta Matsumoto, Henry Segerman, and Steve Trettel
- [Scott Carter's illustrations](https://www.southalabama.edu/mathstat/personal_pages/carter/surfaces.html) - illustrations of surfaces in 4-dimensional space, bright and cheerful!
- [Artworks of Anatolii Fomenko](https://imgur.com/a/vJX89) - depictions of abstract mathematical phenomena, neither bright nor cheerful
