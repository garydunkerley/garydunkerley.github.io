## Teaching statement
I consider teaching to be a core part of my vocation and am committed to constantly improving my teaching in service to my students.

I acknowledge the many systemic problems with mathematics education in the US and my my goals in teaching (heavily influenced by 
[Federico Ardila's](http://math.sfsu.edu/federico/) axioms) seek to address these. 

I think the primary responsibility of math educators is to accurately present mathematics as an useful and learnable skill to the entire spectrum of professional and personal experiences.
This is straightforward for students who are already interested in financial or scientific work, for the remainder (and quite often, the former as well) we must work harder. In my teaching, I emphasize that mathematics is not just a topic in a comprehensive exam or a rare talent for the special few: it is a human endeavor, our common intellectual heritage, and the singular best tool we have for understanding the world and making decisions. 
I encourage students to imagine themselves as capable of mathematical growth through natural processes of curiousity and investigation, regardless of their race, gender, past experiences, or socioeconomic background. 
I see it as my responsibility to do everything I can to push my students, especially those from underpresented backgrounds, to grow mathematically.
