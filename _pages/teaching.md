## My teaching philosophy
I consider teaching to be a core part of my vocation and am committed to constantly improving in service to my students. My goals in teaching are inspired by Federico Ardila's [axioms](http://math.sfsu.edu/federico/) as well as a [famous essay](https://www.goodreads.com/author/show/6421135.Paul_Lockhart) by Paul Lockhart; I seek to address disparities in mathematical literacy, remaining mindful of the inequities frequently underpinning them. 

My primary responsibility as an educator is to accurately present mathematics as a learnable skill, one that is applicable to the entire spectrum of professional and personal experience.
Though it is relatively straightforward to motivate students already interested in financial, engineering, or scientific work, for the remainder (and quite often, the former as well) we must work harder. In my teaching, I emphasize that mathematics is not just a hurdle or a rare talent exclusive to the "special few." Math is a human endeavor; it is a 3,000-year-old intellectual heritage we are all heir to; it is relevant to anyone who needs to understand the world and making decisions (so, everyone). 
I encourage students to imagine themselves as capable of mathematical growth through natural processes of curiousity and investigation. I am mindful of how factors such race, gender, past experiences, or socioeconomic background frequently deprive students of positive mathematical experiences and decent mentors.  
I see it as my responsibility to compassionately push my students, especially those from underpresented backgrounds, to grow but also to desire growth, both mathematically and personally.
