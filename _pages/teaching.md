## My teaching philosophy
I consider teaching to be a core part of my vocation and am committed to constantly improving in service to my students. My goals in teaching are inspired by Federico Ardila's [axioms](http://math.sfsu.edu/federico/)); I seek to address disparities in mathematical literacy as well as the more nebulous sociopolitical inequities underpinning them. 

My primary responsibility as an educator is to accurately present mathematics as a learnable skill, one that is applicable to the entire spectrum of professional and personal experience.
While it might seem straightforward to do so for students already interested in financial or scientific work, for the remainder (and quite often, the former as well) we must work harder. In my teaching, I emphasize that mathematics is not just a topic in a comprehensive exam or a rare talent exclusive to engineers, quantitative analysts, or the "special few." Math is a human endeavor, a 3,000-year-old intellectual heritage we are all heir to, and the singular best tool we have for understanding the world and making decisions. 
I encourage students to imagine themselves as capable of mathematical growth through natural processes of curiousity and investigation, regardless of their race, gender, past experiences, or socioeconomic background.  
I see it as my responsibility to compassionately push my students, especially those from underpresented backgrounds, to grow but also to desire growth, both mathematically and personally.
